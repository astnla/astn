PROFILE

Hi there, my name is Austin Lara! I'm a 25-year-old self-taught small creator who likes to tinker in all forms of media. My roots began with making music and creating things for others to enjoy. It wasn't long before I began teaching myself how to video edit in Adobe Premiere or design in Adobe Photoshop. Granted, to this day, I still consider myself a rookie.

In 2021, I picked up a camera after a trip out to the mountains in Blue Ridge, Georgia and fell in love with photography. Three months later, I made my first video highlighting moments from a work event - the growth is evident.

That leads us to where I am today - A small man, from a small town, working at a school, freezing happy moments with photos & creating timeless memories with videos while enjoying every second doing it.

BACKGROUND

Born in a small Fort Myers, FL hospital, I've lived in Naples, FL my entire life. A son of two very hardworking, supportive, and loving parents, as well as a brother to four siblings.

My childhood plays a big factor in who I am today - music, video games, & my computer. Growing up with so much technology always infatuated me to just learn and create things. I began with designing things for myself at a very young age, as I always wanted to start a YouTube channel.

In high school, I took classes that I felt would better my design process or teach me how to video edit. It wasn't until I began watching other creators online that I truly started to appreciate and develop an eye for cinematography. Creators like Gawx, Gaku Yen, Cole Bennett, BRTHR, & Skymography are some of the many few that inspire my work.

Recently, I started teaching myself how to code with guidance from my uncle, Rodolfo Treviño, a Software Engineering graduate who has always encouraged me to learn throughout my life. Thanks to his support and my determination, I'm proud to say this website is a product of my creativity and self-taught coding skills.

Currently, I'm employed by my local school district and fortunate to work at an elementary school with amazing colleagues and loving students! I count my lucky stars to be able to do what I do—it's not part of my job description, but my administration fully approves and supports it. I was never asked to do this; I simply brought my camera to work one day and filmed an event. The rest was in God's hands.

Even our school district Superintendent, Dr. Leslie Ricciardelli, approached me at a work event that we were hosting! Dr. Ricciardelli complimented me on my work, said how she's seen it all over social media, and went on to say how much she loves it. After our conversation, I got to interview her for that event's video! Take a peak at it here.

I'm lucky enough to be able to say I've found my passion. One might ask, "Is it designing, photography, or videography?" To that, I'd respond that it's much more than that. Instead, I'd say it's making others just feel happy inside through my work and creations. I use my photography & videography to capture those happy moments at our school—moments we can look back to and relive. I've taken my job and mended so much love in my relationships with my colleagues, students, and families. At our school, the bond between staff, students, and families is truly like no other. It feels like we're one big family. It's safe to say that I live and breathe my job.

When I'm creating something, I'm a critical, self-conscious, overthinking perfectionist. I can spend two days editing a 30-second sequence just because the color feels slightly off—but that's the beauty of it! It's not just the thought I put into my work; it's the love. I pour my heart into every detail because I deeply care about the people I'm creating for.

If I'm shooting photos, I try to capture the moment so it'll live forever. When I'm filming and editing, my goal is to create an immersive experience—one that lets you virtually step into the video and relive the moment for those few minutes. My hope is that when you look at my work, you can truly feel that connection.

Thanks for taking the time to learn a bit more about me.
